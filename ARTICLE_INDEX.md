# Article Index

## Purpose of This Document

This document defines the canonical structure of the constitution.

It provides:

• the ordering of articles
• the scope of each article
• relationships between institutional sections
• a stable reference structure for cross-document alignment

All constitutional drafting should remain consistent with this structure unless
explicitly revised through DECISIONS.md.

---

# Structural Ordering Principle

The constitution follows this ordering logic:

1. Purpose
2. Rights
3. Interpretation framework
4. Representation
5. Legislative authority
6. Executive administration
7. Judicial authority
8. Transparency and accountability
9. Emergency authority
10. Amendment process

This sequence reflects the principle:

rights define institutions, and institutions serve the population.

---

# Constitutional Article Structure

## Preamble

Describes:

• the purpose of the constitution
• civic values
• democratic legitimacy principles
• the role of institutions

The preamble provides orientation but does not create enforceable law.

---

## Article I — Fundamental Rights

Defines the rights framework that constrains all institutions.

Includes:

• equality protections
• expression protections
• privacy protections
• digital rights
• informational autonomy
• housing security
• food security
• healthcare security
• protection from discrimination

This article establishes the limits within which institutions operate.

---

## Article II — Constitutional Principles

Defines interpretive guidance for:

• courts
• legislators
• administrators

Clarifies:

• structural intent
• authority boundaries
• democratic legitimacy principles

Transparency is a structural condition of legitimate public authority and applies across all institutions unless constitutionally limited.

These principles guide interpretation of constitutional structure and rights but do not create independent policymaking authority for courts or administrators.

---

## Article III — Representation

Defines how representatives are selected and structured.

Includes:

• district equality requirements
• civic lottery participation mechanisms
• eligibility rules
• rotation expectations
• term limits
• staggered terms

Ensures fair and distributed representation.

---

## Article IV — Legislature

Defines the structure and authority of the legislature.

Includes:

• unicameral structure
• lawmaking authority
• expenditure authority
• oversight authority
• war authorization authority
• administrative delegation authority

Establishes legislative primacy in democratic governance.

---

## Article V — Executive Administration

Defines executive responsibilities and limits.

Includes:

• administrative implementation authority
• relationship to legislature
• coordination of public institutions
• emergency defensive authority limits

Explicitly excludes:

• veto authority
• independent lawmaking authority
• unilateral war authority

---

## Article VI — Judiciary

Defines judicial authority and structure.

Includes:

• constitutional interpretation authority
• judicial review authority
• rights enforcement authority
• highest court structure

Clarifies limits of judicial policymaking authority.

---

## Article VII — Transparency and Accountability

Implements and enforces the constitution’s transparency and accountability requirements across institutions.

Includes:

• public access expectations
• accountability mechanisms
• limits on confidentiality
• oversight visibility principles

---

## Article VIII — Emergency Authority

Defines emergency defensive powers and safeguards.

Includes:

• conditions for emergency action
• legislative review requirements
• duration limits
• judicial compliance review

Prevents permanent emergency governance structures.

---

## Article IX — Constitutional Amendment Process

Defines how constitutional change occurs.

Includes:

• amendment proposal mechanisms
• approval thresholds
• review procedures
• legitimacy safeguards

Ensures adaptability without instability.
