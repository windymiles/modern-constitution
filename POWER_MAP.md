# Power Map

## Purpose of This Document

This document maps how authority flows through the constitutional system.

It identifies:

• sources of legitimacy  
• delegation pathways  
• oversight relationships  
• constraint mechanisms  
• emergency authority limits  

Its purpose is to prevent hidden concentration of power and ensure that
institutional relationships remain transparent and reviewable.


---

# Authority Origin Principle

All public authority originates from the population.

Authority flows through representative institutions and is constrained by
constitutional rights.

Structure:

Population
→ Representation
→ Legislature
→ Executive Administration
→ Public Institutions

Judiciary ensures compliance with constitutional structure and rights.


---

# Core Authority Flow

## Population → Legislature

The legislature derives authority directly from the population through:

• geographically equal districts
• civic lottery selection mechanisms (where implemented)
• staggered representative turnover

The legislature is the primary democratic authority.


---

## Legislature → Law

The legislature holds exclusive authority to:

• create laws
• repeal laws
• authorize expenditures
• approve military deployment beyond immediate defense
• oversee executive administration

No other branch may independently create statutory law.


---

## Legislature → Executive

The executive administers laws enacted by the legislature.

Executive authority is:

delegated  
bounded  
reviewable  

The executive does not possess independent legislative authority.


---

## Legislature → Public Administration

Administrative institutions operate under authority delegated through law.

Administrative authority:

originates in legislation  
remains subject to oversight  
does not exist independently


---

## Legislature → Emergency Authority Review

Emergency defensive action may be initiated by the executive only when:

immediate response is necessary

All emergency authority is subject to:

immediate legislative review
time limitation
constitutional compliance


---

# Executive Authority Structure

Executive authority includes:

• implementation of laws
• coordination of public institutions
• administrative leadership
• emergency defensive response (temporary)

Executive authority does NOT include:

• veto power
• independent lawmaking
• unilateral war declaration
• permanent emergency governance


---

# Judiciary Authority Structure

The judiciary interprets constitutional meaning and ensures legal compliance.

Authority includes:

• constitutional interpretation
• judicial review
• rights protection enforcement

Authority does NOT include:

• legislative policymaking
• executive administration


---

# Highest Court Authority

The highest court provides final constitutional review authority.

Responsibilities include:

• resolving constitutional disputes
• protecting structural boundaries between branches
• safeguarding rights protections

The highest court ensures consistency across the legal system.


---

# Rights Constraint Layer

Rights limit all institutional authority.

Structure:

Population
→ Constitution
→ Rights
→ Institutions

Institutions operate within rights constraints rather than defining them.


---

# Transparency Constraint Layer

Transparency applies across all branches:

Legislature
Executive
Judiciary
Administrative institutions

Transparency exceptions permitted only for:

• personal privacy
• personnel matters
• legitimate national security needs


---

# Civic Lottery Authority Distribution Layer

Civic lottery mechanisms distribute representative authority broadly across
the population.

This reduces:

• party gatekeeping
• career political entrenchment
• elite capture risk

Lottery-selected representatives hold equal institutional authority while serving.


---

# Oversight Relationships

Oversight ensures authority remains accountable.

Structure:

Legislature
→ oversees executive administration

Judiciary
→ reviews constitutional compliance of laws and executive actions

Population
→ authorizes representative legitimacy through institutional structure


---

# Administrative Authority Boundaries

Administrative institutions:

implement policy  
execute delegated authority  
operate transparently  

They do not:

create law independently
exercise permanent autonomous authority
override legislative direction


---

# Emergency Authority Map

Emergency defensive authority flows:

Population
→ Legislature (primary war authority)

Executive
→ temporary defensive response (limited)

Legislature
→ immediate review authority

Judiciary
→ constitutional compliance review


---

# Anti-Concentration Safeguards

Authority concentration risks are mitigated through:

• legislative primacy
• absence of executive veto power
• judicial review
• civic lottery participation pathways
• staggered representative turnover
• transparency requirements


---

# Structural Redundancy Layer

Key protections are supported by multiple institutions.

Examples:

Executive constraint supported by:

• legislative oversight
• judicial review
• transparency obligations
• emergency authority limits


Rights protection supported by:

• constitutional structure
• judiciary enforcement
• transparency mechanisms
• representative oversight


Representation integrity supported by:

• district equality
• civic lottery pathways
• staggered terms
• term limits
