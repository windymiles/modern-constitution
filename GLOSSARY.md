# Glossary

## Purpose of This Document

This document defines key constitutional terms used throughout the project.

Defined terms should be used consistently across:

• constitutional articles  
• commentary  
• institutional models  
• guardrails  
• power maps  
• cross-reference maps  

If a new technical term becomes necessary, it should be added here before use elsewhere.


---

# Core Constitutional Terms


## Constitution

The foundational legal framework defining institutional structure, authority relationships, rights protections, and amendment procedures.

The constitution establishes structure, not ordinary law.


## Article

A primary structural component of the constitution defining a major institutional or rights domain.


## Section

A subdivision of an article addressing a specific institutional rule or principle.


## Commentary

The official interpretive companion document explaining the intent and reasoning behind constitutional provisions.

Commentary supports interpretation but does not replace constitutional text.

# Institutional Structure Terms


## Legislature

The representative lawmaking body of the constitutional system responsible for:

• creating laws  
• approving expenditures  
• authorizing war  
• overseeing the executive branch


## Representative

An individual selected to exercise legislative authority on behalf of the population.


## District

A geographic representation unit with approximately equal population used to ensure equal representation in the legislature.


## Executive

The branch responsible for administering and implementing laws enacted by the legislature.

The executive does not possess independent lawmaking authority.


## Prime Minister

The head of the executive branch responsible for coordinating administration and implementing legislative policy under legislative oversight.


## Judiciary

The system of courts responsible for interpreting the constitution and ensuring laws remain consistent with constitutional structure and rights protections.


## Highest Court

The court with final authority over constitutional interpretation and judicial review.


---

# Authority and Oversight Terms


## Judicial Review

The authority of courts to determine whether laws or government actions comply with the constitution.


## Legislative Oversight

The authority of the legislature to monitor, evaluate, and constrain executive administration and public agencies.


## Administrative Authority

Authority delegated to executive institutions for implementing laws within limits established by the legislature.


## Emergency Authority

Temporary authority granted to respond to immediate threats, subject to strict time limits and legislative review.


---

# Representation Terms


## Civic Lottery

A selection process in which eligible members of the population are randomly chosen to serve in representative roles.

Civic lotteries reduce:

• party capture  
• career political entrenchment  
• elite gatekeeping


## Staggered Terms

A system in which representatives begin and end service at different times to preserve institutional continuity.


## Term Limit

A restriction on the number or duration of terms an individual may serve in a representative role.

# Rights Terms


## Fundamental Rights

Basic protections guaranteed by the constitution that limit government authority and protect individual dignity and autonomy.


## Equality

Equal treatment under the law regardless of identity characteristics including:

• sex  
• gender identity  
• sexual orientation  
• ethnicity  
• religion  
• disability status


## Digital Privacy

Protection against unauthorized surveillance, collection, or misuse of personal digital information.


## Informational Autonomy

The right of individuals to control the collection, use, and disclosure of personal data about themselves.


## Housing Security

Protection against involuntary loss of access to safe and stable shelter.


## Food Security

Reliable access to sufficient and nutritious food necessary for health and dignity.


## Healthcare Security

Reliable access to essential medical services necessary to maintain health and well-being.

# Governance Principles


## Transparency

The default condition in which government actions, decisions, and records are accessible to the public unless restricted for legitimate reasons.


## Legitimate Confidentiality

Restrictions on transparency permitted only for:

• personal privacy  
• personnel matters  
• narrowly defined national security needs


## Institutional Resilience

The capacity of constitutional institutions to remain stable under political conflict, crisis, or adversarial behavior.


## Structural Safeguard

A rule or mechanism designed to prevent concentration of power or institutional abuse regardless of individual intentions.


## Structural Redundancy

The use of multiple independent safeguards protecting the same democratic principle so that failure of one safeguard does not compromise the system.


## Constitutional Minimalism

The principle that constitutional text defines structure and authority relationships while leaving operational detail to supporting frameworks and legislation.

# Risk and Guardrail Terms


## Institutional Capture

A condition in which public institutions serve the interests of a narrow group rather than the population as a whole.


## Executive Consolidation

Expansion of executive authority beyond administrative implementation into legislative or constitutional domains.


## Legislative Cartelization

Control of legislative access or decision-making by entrenched political parties or elite actors.


## Bureaucratic Entrenchment

Accumulation of durable governing authority within administrative institutions insulated from democratic oversight.


## Judicial Overreach

Exercise of judicial authority beyond constitutional interpretation into policymaking functions.


## Oligarchic Influence

Disproportionate control of political outcomes by concentrated economic or social elites.

# Project Architecture Terms


## Decision Locking

A rule preventing modification of decisions marked STABLE or FOUNDATIONAL without explicit reconsideration.


## Foundational Decision

A core architectural commitment that shapes multiple institutional components and cannot change without major redesign.


## Stress Test

A scenario used to evaluate whether institutional safeguards remain effective under crisis conditions.


## Failure Path

A historically observed sequence through which democratic systems lose stability or legitimacy.


## Authority Flow

The structured movement of decision-making power between institutions as described in POWER_MAP.md.


## Dependency Relationship

A structural connection in which one constitutional component relies on another for interpretation or implementation.
