# Article II — Constitutional Principles

## Section 1 — Purpose of Constitutional Principles

This Article defines principles governing interpretation of the Constitution and the exercise of public authority.

These principles bind:

• the legislature
• the executive
• the judiciary
• administrative institutions
• all public officials exercising constitutional authority

They guide interpretation of all Articles and apply wherever constitutional meaning is uncertain.

---

## Section 2 — Rights-First Interpretation

Fundamental rights define the purpose and limits of public institutions.

No institution may interpret this Constitution in a manner that weakens or redefines the rights guaranteed in Article I.

Where multiple interpretations are plausible, the interpretation that best preserves rights protections shall prevail.

---

## Section 3 — Source of Democratic Authority

All public authority originates from the population.

Representative authority flows through institutions established by this Constitution and is exercised primarily through the legislature.

No institution may claim independent governing authority outside this constitutional structure.

---

## Section 4 — Legislative Primacy

The legislature is the primary democratic lawmaking authority.

Interpretation of this Constitution shall not:

transfer lawmaking authority away from the legislature
permit executive creation of law
permit judicial substitution of policy judgment for legislative decision-making

Delegated authority must remain subject to legislative direction and oversight.

---

## Section 5 — Administrative Role of the Executive

The executive administers and implements laws enacted by the legislature.

Executive authority:

• derives from legislation
• remains subject to legislative oversight
• shall not include independent lawmaking authority

Emergency defensive authority may be exercised only within the limits defined by this Constitution.

---

## Section 6 — Role of the Judiciary

The judiciary interprets this Constitution and ensures compliance with its requirements.

Judicial authority includes:

• constitutional interpretation
• review of institutional compliance
• protection of rights guaranteed by this Constitution

Judicial authority does not include creation of public policy or expansion of institutional powers beyond those established by this Constitution.

Where constitutional change is required, amendment procedures defined in Article IX shall be used.

---

## Section 7 — Transparency as a Condition of Legitimate Authority

Transparency is the default condition of constitutional governance.

Public authority shall be exercised openly except where confidentiality is necessary to protect:

• personal privacy
• personnel matters
• legitimate national security interests

Confidentiality shall be limited in scope and duration and shall remain subject to oversight.

Opacity shall not be used to insulate institutions from democratic accountability.

---

## Section 8 — Preservation of Distributed Authority

Interpretation of this Constitution shall preserve the distribution of authority among representative, administrative, and judicial institutions.

No interpretation shall be adopted that increases risk of:

• executive consolidation
• legislative cartelization
• bureaucratic entrenchment
• judicial overreach
• oligarchic influence
• party monopoly over representation

Where multiple interpretations are possible, preference shall be given to interpretations that strengthen democratic accountability and institutional balance.

---

## Section 9 — Structural Consistency Requirement

Interpretation of this Constitution shall remain consistent with its institutional structure as defined across its Articles.

Supporting constitutional framework documents, including COMMENTARY, POWER_MAP, SYSTEM_GUARDRAILS, CROSS_REFERENCE_MAP, DECISIONS, and INSTITUTIONAL_STRESS_TESTS, form part of the constitutional interpretive framework and may be used to preserve coherence of interpretation and prevent structural drift, provided they remain consistent with this Constitution.

---

## Section 10 — Continuity of Constitutional Authority During Emergencies

Emergency defensive authority permits temporary executive response to immediate threats but does not suspend the constitutional structure of representative or judicial oversight.

Emergency authority shall not:

• transfer sovereign authority to the executive
• suspend legislative authority
• limit judicial review authority
• replace representative decision-making with executive discretion
• dissolve or bypass constitutional institutions

Legislative review and judicial compliance review shall remain continuously operative during emergency conditions.

Emergency authority shall be interpreted as temporary, limited, and subject to constitutional constraint at all times.
